## What's the difference between a list and a dict? When do you use each?

A **list** is an ordered collection of items where each item is accessed by its position (index).
You use a list when the order matters or when you just have a sequence of values, like a list of scores, names, or steps.

A **dict** is a collection of key-value pairs where each item is accessed by a meaningful key instead of a position.
You use a dict when you want to label your data, like storing a student's name, subject, and scores together, or counting how many times each word appears.

In short: use a **list** when you have *a bunch of the same kind of thing*, use a **dict** when you want to *describe something with named fields*.
